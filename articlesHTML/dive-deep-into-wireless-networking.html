Wireless Networking Deep Dive

In today's world, wireless networking has become a crucial component of both personal and professional environments. Understanding the intricacies of Wireless LAN (WLAN) design and deployment options can significantly enhance network performance and reliability. This article delves into the considerations for WLAN design, various deployment options, and the underlying theory of wireless communications.

WLAN Design Considerations

Wireless Deployment Options

When designing a wireless network, selecting the right type of Access Point (AP) is essential. There are primarily two types of APs to consider: Autonomous and Lightweight.

  • Autonomous Access Points (APs):

    • These are standalone, independent devices.
    • Ideal for home or small office environments.
    • They operate under a controller-less deployment model.
    • Due to their limited scalability, they are not commonly used in large environments.

  • Lightweight Access Points (APs):

    • These require a central Wireless LAN Controller (WLC).
    • Operate under a controller-based deployment model.
    • WLCs can be physical or virtual.
    • The controller manages and communicates changes to the APs.
    • They use the Control and Provisioning of Wireless Access Points (CAPWAP) protocol, which facilitates communication between APs and the WLC.

Ways to Deploy

There are several deployment models to consider based on network requirements and scale:

  • Cisco FlexConnect:

    • Enables configuration and control of remote wireless networks.
    • Similar to Layer 3 roaming with CAPWAP.

  • Central Switched:

    • The standard CAPWAP mode of operation.
    • Typically not recommended due to potential performance bottlenecks.

  • Local Switched:

    • Maps user traffic to a VLAN on an adjacent switch.
    • Control and management traffic are still sent over CAPWAP to the WLC.

Location Services

Location services have become an integral part of modern WLANs, providing various use cases:

  • Enterprise Asset Tracking: Helps organizations keep track of valuable assets.
  • Location-Based Advertising: Delivers targeted advertisements based on the user's location.

Received Signal Strength (RSS): Measured in dBm, RSS can be used to triangulate a device's location, enhancing the precision of location-based services.

Cisco Solutions for Location Services

Cisco offers several solutions to implement robust location services:

  • Real-time Location Services (RTLS): Provides real-time tracking and location analytics.
  • Cisco DNA Spaces: A comprehensive platform for indoor location services.
  • Cisco Meraki Platform: Suitable for medium-sized environments, offering seamless location tracking and management.

WLAN Design Options

Ad Hoc Wireless LAN

An Ad Hoc wireless LAN allows devices to communicate directly with each other without needing an AP, suitable for quick, temporary networks.

Other WLAN Designs

  • Infrastructure Wireless LAN: Features an AP connected to a switch via Ethernet, providing a robust and scalable network.
  • Mesh Wireless LAN: Useful when devices are not near an Ethernet port; they can connect to another AP, creating a mesh network.
